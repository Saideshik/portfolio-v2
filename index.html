About me

I'm a Security Engineer focused on detection engineering, cloud security, and DevSecOps. My work sits at the intersection of building systems and breaking them — designing the controls that catch real attackers, not just check compliance boxes.

At IN TECHNET, I owned detection engineering and incident response across 120+ cloud and on-prem assets spanning AWS and Kubernetes. I authored Splunk correlation rules for credential stuffing and brute-force attacks (ATT&CK T1110), embedded SonarQube and OWASP ZAP into CI/CD pipelines blocking 90% of critical vulnerabilities, and hardened AWS workloads with GuardDuty, CloudTrail, and least-privilege IAM.

Currently I'm pursuing my M.S. in Cybersecurity at Saint Louis University (GPA 3.97) and working in ITS, where I review SSH brute-force patterns, enforce key-only authentication, and triage Qualys/Nessus findings against asset criticality.

Outside of work, I build security tooling — ThreatWatch, my AI-driven detection & response platform, ingests Wazuh alerts, maps to ATT&CK techniques, and uses agentic AI to auto-generate triage steps. I'm AWS Certified Security – Specialty and Security+ certified, and I compete in CTFs (DEF CON, Amazon CTF, NCL) when I get the chance.
